Flat Embossed faces are flat plastic faces (no pan) with raised letters and/or shapes. The embossing adds strength and a decorative element to faces that are too small to be panned. The embossing depth is determined by the stroke and size of the letter or shape. The raised objects are cut with a 7 degree draft to allow the plastic to release from the mold.

General embossed copy heights:
4" to 5" are embossed ¼ inch.
5” or taller will be embossed ½ inch.
Letters under 4” cannot be embossed.
Letter strokes less than ½ inch wide cannot be embossed.

Note: We do not recommend using a flat face when the lamps in the face will be closer than 4" from the sign face. If the lamps are too close to the face you may see the image of the bulbs and the heat from the lamps may cause problems to the face. We also do not recommend using flat faces in cases where the dimensions are greater than 3 feet tall or the face is more than 32 square feet. A flat face loses its strength as it becomes larger and runs the risk of being blown out of the cabinet.
